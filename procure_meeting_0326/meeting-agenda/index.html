    Meeting Agenda: Henrik & Sergio¶

    Purpose

    Key discussion topics based on all empirical work conducted on the NLL identification strategy. Organized by decision urgency.

    1. What is the paper's empirical contribution?¶

    We now have a complete portfolio of results. The fundamental question is: what story do we tell?

    Option A: "Institutional rules reshape corruption composition"¶

    The strongest framing, supported by:

    • Sign test: 7/8 Santos & Souza predictions correct (p = 0.035)
    • Decreto 9.412 placebo: 3/3 correct, all p < 0.001
    • Enforcement null (Pillar 1): MDE within literature range, confirming rules > monitoring
    • Size heterogeneity: waterbed in small municipalities (p = 0.089)

    Weakness: no individual scheme outcome significant at 5%.

    Option B: "The NLL has no detectable causal effect on procurement"¶

    The honest framing if we weight individual significance:

    • Process-level DiD (28K processes): all null
    • Municipality-year CS: all individual outcomes null at 5%
    • HonestDiD: all CIs include zero

    Weakness: contradicts the sign test (p = 0.035) and Decreto 9.412 (p < 0.001).

    Option C: Hybrid — "We can predict the direction but not the magnitude"¶

    Most defensible:

    "The mechanism taxonomy correctly predicts which corruption types will increase and decrease under institutional reform (sign test p = 0.035), but the individual effects are too small or too early to detect given audit lag. The Decreto 9.412 proof-of-concept confirms the mechanism operates at massive scale (p < 0.001) for a comparable reform."

    Decision needed

    Which framing does Henrik prefer? Option C requires careful positioning relative to the "null result" literature. The sign test needs to be presented as a joint test of the taxonomy, not as a workaround for individual nulls.

    2. The sign test: strength or weakness?¶

    What we have¶

    Test p-value
    Binomial (7/8, p = 0.5) 0.035
    Stouffer unweighted 0.060
    Permutation (shuffled labels) 0.071
    Fisher combination 0.158

    The tension¶

    The binomial (p = 0.035) is significant. The permutation (p = 0.071) is not. The difference arises because bunching outcomes are correlated (same distribution of dispensas).

    Options¶

    1. Report binomial as primary, permutation as robustness — defensible if we argue predictions are independent (bunching uses despesa data, schemes use DOE/TCE)
    2. Report Stouffer (p = 0.060) as the single test — accounts for magnitude, not just direction
    3. Drop the sign test entirely — rely on Decreto 9.412 (p < 0.001) + individual margins

    Decision needed

    Is a sign test at p = 0.035–0.071 strong enough for the paper Henrik wants to write? Or should we pivot to the Decreto 9.412 as the main result and treat the NLL scheme composition as "early, directionally consistent evidence"?

    3. The Decreto 9.412: how central should it be?¶

    The Decreto 9.412 threshold change (2018) produces our cleanest, strongest result: bunching migration with p < 0.001, 30+ million transactions, visually dramatic density shift.

    Current placement: robustness/placebo¶

    Proposed placement: Section 1 of empirical analysis¶

    Structure:

    1. "Threshold changes cause bunching migration" (Decreto 9.412, p < 0.001)
    2. "The NLL produces the same pattern, plus predicted scheme composition shifts" (sign test, p = 0.035)
    3. "Enforcement does not produce these shifts" (Pillar 1 null)

    For Sergio

    The Decreto 9.412 is a Lei 8.666 threshold adjustment, not an NLL event. Does it belong in the NLL section of the paper, or in a separate "mechanism validation" section? How does it interact with the identification brief's diff-in-discontinuity proposal?

    4. Pillar 1 null: feature or bug?¶

    The result¶

    The relator IV instrument is strong (first stage: +38 pp diligências, p < 0.001) but the reduced form on procurement composition is null. Power calculation shows MDE = 3.4 pp for pregão — within the literature range (Ferraz & Finan: 3–5 pp).

    The narrative¶

    "Enforcement intensity affects the probability of detection but not the type of procurement. This is consistent with the mechanism taxonomy: scheme choice is determined by institutional constraints, not monitoring pressure."

    The risk¶

    A referee could say: "Your Pillar 1 is just underpowered. The MDE of 12.9 pp for dispensa is too large to be informative."

    Decision needed

    Do we present the Pillar 1 null as:

    • A validated null (enforcement ≠ scheme choice) → requires defending the MDE
    • A methodological contribution (clean IV, institutional setting) → reduced form secondary
    • Dropped from the main paper → focus on Pillar 2 only

    5. Process-level DiD: what does the null mean?¶

    The result¶

    28,792 processes with municipality × year FE. NLL and Lei 8.666 processes within the same municipality-year are identical on all observables (value, bidders, spending).

    Interpretations¶

    1. The NLL doesn't change procurement at the process level — it changes the institutional environment in which corruption operates (our argument)
    2. The process-level data lacks the right outcomes — we need edital text, qualification clauses, amendment rates (data not yet available)
    3. Selection bias — which processes get done under NLL vs 8.666 within the same municipality is not random

    For Henrik

    Should we present the process-level null in the paper? It addresses the referee's #1 priority but the null is hard to spin positively. Alternative: frame in the appendix with a note that "process-level observables are uninformative about corruption composition."

    7. Paper architecture: two or three pillars?¶

    Current: Two pillars + reserve¶

    Pillar Channel Status Strength
    1. Relator IV Enforcement Validated null Strong instrument, informative null
    2. NLL Reform Institutional rules Sign test p = 0.035 Strongest causal evidence
    3. Close-election RDD Political actors Weak first stage Feasible but mechanism unclear

    Pillar 3 assessment¶

    • 125 close elections at 10% margin — sufficient N
    • But: Bolsonaro alignment ≠ NLL adoption speed (no first stage)
    • Donor-contract pipeline: dead (corporate donations banned 2015)

    Options¶

    1. Two pillars only (enforcement + rules) — cleaner paper, easier to defend
    2. Two pillars + heterogeneity — use alignment as a moderator, not an instrument
    3. Three pillars — keep RDD but reframe as testing political alignment effects on procurement (not via NLL adoption speed)

    Decision needed

    Henrik's identification brief proposed three pillars. Given that Pillar 3's first stage doesn't work, do we:

    • Drop it and write a two-pillar paper?
    • Reframe alignment as heterogeneity analysis?
    • Find a different instrument for the political channel?

    9. Summary: the three things we need to decide¶

    Decision 1: Paper framing

    Is this a paper about null effects that are directionally consistent (Option C), or a paper that validates the mechanism taxonomy via the sign test (Option A)? This determines everything else.

    Decision 2: Decreto 9.412 placement

    Main result (Figure 1) or robustness check? If main result, the paper structure changes fundamentally — we lead with mechanism validation, not with the NLL.

    Decision 3: Number of pillars

    Two (enforcement + rules) or three (+ political actors)? Pillar 3's first stage is weak. Cutting it simplifies the paper substantially.
