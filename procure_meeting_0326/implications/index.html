    Implications¶

    For the identification strategy¶

    Pre-trends¶

    All seven outcomes pass the joint Wald pre-trend test under CS. The TWFE pre-trend violations for share_pregao_eletronico were artifacts of forbidden comparisons, not genuine differential trends. This validates the staggered adoption design when properly estimated.

    Selection into treatment¶

    The balance table reveals that never-treated municipalities are fundamentally different from treated ones:

    • 10× fewer processes (11.6 vs. 75–149 per year)
    • Nearly zero pregão eletrônico (1.1% vs. 11–12%)
    • Twice the tomada de preços share (11.9% vs. 6%)

    This raises concerns about extrapolation: the never-treated comparison group may not be a valid counterfactual for treated municipalities. The not-yet-treated specification (Spec D) partially addresses this, and results are qualitatively similar — but this robustness check is not dispositive.

    Forbidden comparisons¶

    The TWFE estimates for share_pregao_eletronico showed spurious significance:

    Estimator ATT at k=+2 p-value
    TWFE +0.088 0.022**
    Callaway-Sant'Anna +0.017 0.527

    The CS estimate is 5× smaller and insignificant. This is a textbook demonstration of why heterogeneity-robust estimators are necessary with staggered adoption.

    Power¶

    With 65 never-treated municipalities and most cohorts having 1–3 post-treatment years, the study is substantially underpowered.

    Minimum detectable effect

    A back-of-envelope calculation suggests we are powered to detect effects of approximately 5–8 percentage points in modality shares. The observed point estimates (1–3 pp) are well below this threshold. The null results are therefore not informative about the absence of effects — they are consistent with both zero effects and moderate effects masked by noise.

    For the paper¶

    Defensible results¶

    The only outcome with a theoretically motivated, directionally correct, and marginally significant effect is share_convite (ATT = −0.029, p = 0.089). The NLL eliminates convite as a modality, so this is a mechanical effect that should emerge with sufficient data. The group-level decomposition shows the effect is concentrated in cohort 2022 (p = 0.053), the largest cohort.

    However, this result does not survive HonestDiD sensitivity analysis, even at \(\bar{M} = 0\).

    Results needing caveats¶

    mean_n_bidders shows the expected positive direction (+2.0 bidders) but is far from significant (p = 0.291). This could be highlighted as suggestive but underpowered evidence that NLL's electronic default increases competition.

    Results to drop or move to appendix¶

    share_pregao_eletronico should not be presented with TWFE results; only CS results should be shown. The TWFE comparison can serve as a methodological illustration of forbidden comparisons in the appendix.

    Strengthening analyses¶

    1. Extend to 2025 data — adds one post-treatment year for all cohorts
    2. Process-level outcomes — dramatically increases N and power (from ~4,000 to ~350,000 observations)
    3. Add population/GDP controls — addresses baseline imbalance
    4. Synthetic control or matching — constructs a more credible comparison group from the late-adopters pool
    5. Alternative treatment definition — use Audesp-internal signals (NLL-only modalities like "Dispensa Eletrônica") instead of PNCP

    Honest assessment¶

    Strongest finding

    The TWFE vs. CS comparison is itself a valuable methodological contribution. It demonstrates, in a real-world procurement setting, how forbidden comparisons generate spurious treatment effects. The pregão eletrônico TWFE result (ATT = +0.088, p = 0.022) completely vanishes under CS (ATT = +0.017, p = 0.527).

    Biggest threat to validity

    The never-treated comparison group (65 municipalities, 11.6 mean annual processes) is fundamentally different from treated municipalities. These are very small, less professionalized governments that may have different procurement dynamics regardless of NLL adoption.

    Top field journal referee concern

    "The paper presents null results from a staggered DiD with a problematic comparison group. The never-treated units are an order of magnitude smaller than treated units. The study is clearly underpowered — can the authors provide a power calculation? With 65 comparison municipalities and 1–3 post-treatment years, what is the minimum detectable effect size? If the study cannot detect economically meaningful effects, the null results are uninformative and the analysis should be reframed as descriptive rather than causal."
