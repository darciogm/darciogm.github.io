    4. Recommendation¶

    Major Revision

    The paper addresses a first-order question with a genuinely novel combination of data and methods. The mechanism-labeled corruption dataset is a real contribution, and the theoretical framework provides useful structure.

    However, the paper does not yet deliver a convincing causal result:

    • The relator IV produces mostly null reduced forms
    • The NLL reform test is unimplemented
    • The LLM validation is incomplete
    • The process-level sample (N = 56) is too thin for the sharpest tests

    These are not fatal problems --- the data and institutional knowledge exist to address all of them --- but they require substantial work.

    What Would Change the Verdict¶

    Scenario Verdict
    Clean NLL compositional result + strengthened relator IV + completed LLM validation Accept at top-5
    NLL results null + relator IV still marginal + LLM validated Strong field journal (JEEA, REStat, JDE)
    No new causal results + LLM validated + descriptives polished Mid-tier field journal
    Current state (no NLL, marginal IV, no validation) Reject at top-5

    The Core Issue¶

    The paper has more theory than identification and more data than results. The 12 hypotheses, 5 theoretical frameworks, and 14 clean parquet tables represent an extraordinary investment in infrastructure. But a top-5 publication requires converting that infrastructure into one or two well-identified, clearly stated findings. The paper is currently trying to be everything --- typology paper, causal paper, detection paper, network paper, measurement paper --- and needs to decide what it is.
