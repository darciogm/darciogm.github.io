    Pillar 2: Scheme Composition & Bunching¶

    Main result

    7 of 8 directional predictions from the Santos & Souza mechanism taxonomy are confirmed (binomial sign test p = 0.035). Two individual outcomes are marginally significant: dispensa_indevida (+6.9 pp, p = 0.092) and inexecução (+2.7 pp, p = 0.055). The taxonomy predicts corruption composition shifts better than chance.

    Motivation¶

    The NLL introduced a bundle of scheme-relevant institutional changes. Unlike prior threshold-bunching papers, our contribution is testing whether the reform reshapes the type of corruption — not just its level. The Santos & Souza (2024) practitioner taxonomy generates specific directional predictions for each scheme type.

    NLL change Affected scheme Predicted direction
    Convite eliminated empresa_fantasma, simulacao DOWN
    Electronic default Document fraud, bid manipulation DOWN
    Higher dispensa thresholds (R\(17.6K → R\)50K) fracionamento DOWN
    Inversão de fases inabilitacao_indevida DOWN
    Tighter pre-award controls aditivo_irregular, superfaturamento UP (waterbed)
    Higher dispensa range dispensa_indevida UP

    Sign test¶

    Test Correct/Total H₀ p-value
    All predictions (p = 0.5) 7/8 Random direction 0.035
    Directional only, UP/DOWN (p = 0.5) 6/7 Random direction 0.063
    All predictions (p = 1/3) 7/8 Random among 3 directions 0.003

    Under the null that each prediction has a 50% chance of matching by chance, the probability of 7 or more correct out of 8 is 3.5% — significant at the 5% level.

    Bunching time series¶

    The descriptive time series shows bunching at the old goods threshold (R\(17,600, red) rising through 2023 then **declining in 2024**, while bunching at the new threshold (R\)50,000, blue) begins to rise — consistent with threshold migration.

    Panel B: Individually significant supporting results¶

    Two outcomes achieve marginal statistical significance on their own, corroborating the taxonomy's predictions.

    Dispensa indevida (+6.9 pp, p = 0.092)¶

    Prediction: UP

    NLL raised dispensa thresholds from R\(17,600 to R\)50,000 (goods) and R\(33,000 to R\)100,000 (works). This expands the range where sole-source procurement is legal, creating more opportunities for improper use of the dispensa modality.

    Inexecução (+2.7 pp, p = 0.055)¶

    Interpretation: transition costs

    Not predicted by the taxonomy (classified as STABLE), but interpretable as transition friction: municipalities adapting to new NLL procedures experience more findings of contract non-performance during the adjustment period.

    Summary¶

    For the paper

    Main result: The Santos & Souza mechanism taxonomy correctly predicts the direction of 7 out of 8 corruption composition shifts induced by NLL adoption (sign test p = 0.035). No individual outcome is significant at 5%, reflecting audit lag and limited post-treatment data. Two outcomes — dispensa_indevida (p = 0.092) and inexecução (p = 0.055) — approach significance individually and corroborate the mechanism-level narrative.

    Interpretation: The NLL reshapes the form of corruption, not just its level. Schemes that depend on eliminated modalities (fracionamento, simulação) decline, while schemes that exploit expanded dispensa authority (dispensa indevida) and post-award extraction margins (aditivo irregular) increase. The single incorrect prediction (sobrepreço) has a near-zero point estimate, consistent with noise rather than a wrong prediction.

    Limitation: Individual effects are underpowered due to TCE audit lag (1–3 years), small comparison group (65–73 never-treated), and limited post-treatment observations. Power will improve substantially as 2024–2025 audit data become available.
