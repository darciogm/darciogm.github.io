    Procure — Darcio's Work¶

    Project: The Organization of Corruption in Public Procurement Authors: Sergio Galletta (ETH Zurich), Darcio Genicolo Martins (Insper), Henrik Sigstad (BI Norwegian Business School) Last updated: March 2026

    What this site contains¶

    This site consolidates all empirical work by Darcio on the NLL identification strategy (Pillar 2), including data pipelines, estimation results, referee simulations, and discussion topics for co-authors.

    Key findings¶

    Mechanism taxonomy validated (sign test p = 0.035)

    7 of 8 directional predictions from the Santos & Souza (2024) mechanism taxonomy are confirmed under NLL adoption. The taxonomy predicts corruption composition shifts better than chance.

    Decreto 9.412 placebo: p < 0.001

    The 2018 threshold change produces massive bunching migration (3/3 predictions correct). This validates the mechanism with an independent reform event and should be the paper's first empirical result.

    Individual outcomes underpowered

    No individual scheme outcome is significant at 5%. Two approach significance: dispensa_indevida (+6.9 pp, p = 0.092) and inexecução (+2.7 pp, p = 0.055). Audit lag and small comparison group (65 never-treated) are the bottlenecks.

    Process-level DiD: informative null

    28,792 NLL vs Lei 8.666 processes within the same municipality-year show no difference on observables. The NLL changes corruption composition, not process-level efficiency.

    Three decisions needed¶

    1. Paper framing

    Is this a paper about validating the mechanism taxonomy (sign test p = 0.035 + Decreto 9.412 p < 0.001)? Or about null effects that are directionally consistent?

    2. Decreto 9.412 placement

    Main result (Figure 1) or robustness check? Leading with mechanism validation changes the paper structure.

    3. Number of pillars

    Two (enforcement + rules) or three (+ political actors)? Pillar 3 first stage is weak (Bolsonaro alignment ≠ NLL adoption speed). Donor pipeline infeasible (corporate donations banned 2015).
