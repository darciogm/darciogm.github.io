# Supplier leverage reappears in thinner and earlier markets

🟡 The deep-market within-firm null is not uniform across the urgent panel.
Splitting the within firm-buyer-item comparison by market depth and timing,
the administrative urgent coefficient is essentially zero where trade is deep
— above-median quantity **−0.005**, SUS-formulary items **−0.001** — but
turns sharply positive where markets are thin or relationships are young:
below-median quantity **+0.066\*\*\***, earlier-period trades
**+0.117\*\*\***, and non-formulary items **+0.101**
([AN-003](../analyses/an-003-within-firm-pricing.md),
[AN-004](../analyses/an-004-market-depth-heterogeneity.md)).

The gradient is the substantive content of the heterogeneity: same-firm
pricing power surfaces exactly where the buyer has the fewest repeated
alternatives and the least history with the supplier. Thin product lines,
early relationships, and items outside the SUS formulary are the segments in
which an urgent buyer is most exposed to the incumbent supplier, and these
are the cells where the urgency premium loads onto the same-firm price. The
deep-market null reported in
[No broad same-firm markup](no-broad-same-firm-markup.md) and the thin-market
leverage documented here are two readings of the same heterogeneity table.

**Caveat.** These are conditional contrasts within the selected administrative
urgent channel — the closest feasible urgent-procurement comparison — not
causal estimates of urgency in thin markets. The market-depth and timing
splits are observational cuts, and "thin", "earlier", and "non-formulary"
cells differ on more dimensions than depth alone, so the positive
coefficients mix leverage with composition. The reading is 🟡 because it is a
single-source own-project estimate in São Paulo BEC.
