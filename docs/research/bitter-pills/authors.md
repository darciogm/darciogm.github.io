## Darcio Genicolo-Martins

**Corresponding Author**

Darcio Genicolo-Martins is a researcher at Insper Institute of Education and Research in Sao Paulo, Brazil. His research focuses on the intersection of public procurement, health policy, and institutional economics. He studies how judicial interventions and regulatory frameworks shape the efficiency of public spending, with particular emphasis on the Brazilian health system.

His work combines large-scale administrative data with econometric methods to evaluate the costs and consequences of institutional design choices in public procurement. The "Bitter Pills" project represents his primary research line on the fiscal implications of health litigation in Brazil.

## Paulo Furquim de Azevedo

Paulo Furquim de Azevedo is a Professor at Insper Institute of Education and Research in Sao Paulo, Brazil. He is a leading scholar in industrial organization, competition policy, and institutional economics in Latin America.

His research agenda spans several areas of applied microeconomics, including public procurement design, antitrust economics, regulation, and the economic analysis of legal institutions. He has published extensively on how institutional frameworks affect market outcomes and public sector efficiency in developing countries.

Professor Furquim de Azevedo has served as an advisor to government agencies and international organizations on competition policy and procurement reform. His work on public procurement in Brazil has contributed to the understanding of how legal and administrative institutions shape the cost-effectiveness of public spending.
