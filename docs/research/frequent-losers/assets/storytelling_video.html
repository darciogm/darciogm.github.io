Overpricing
When governments buy goods through public tenders, firms compete on price. But sometimes firms secretly agree on who wins — forming cartels that inflate prices at taxpayers' expense.
The Detection Gap
Existing bid-rigging screens require detailed bid amounts. But many procurement systems — especially in developing countries — only record who bid and who won.

Where to Look?
A Strange Pattern
Some firms bid on dozens of tenders over a decade and never win a single one. Participating costs time and money. If you never win, why keep showing up?
Cover Bidders
These frequent losers aren't trying to win. They exist to fake competition. Brazilian law requires at least 3 bidders — cartels need extras to fill the seats. Cover bidders submit intentionally high bids and lose on purpose, receiving side payments.

The price premium is larger where cover bidding is voluntary — exactly what strategic deployment predicts. The FL screen also captures information orthogonal to bid-level tools (correlation: 0.06).

Apply detailed bid-level analysis only where risk is highest
Triage, not conviction. Direct scarce investigative resources where the risk is greatest and oversight is weakest.
