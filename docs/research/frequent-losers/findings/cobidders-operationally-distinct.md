# FL cobidders are operationally distinct from other frequent losers

!!! info "Pending finding"
    Scaffold for a result reported in §5 of the
    [manuscript](../paper.md) ("The Economic Profile of Cartel-Adjacent
    Losers").

🟡 Inside the FL14 stratum, **cobidders look different from other always-
losers** along four economically meaningful dimensions:

1. **Buyer breadth.** Cobidders are deployed across more buyers and
   tender-items than non-cobidder FLs.
2. **Product concentration.** They operate in more concentrated product
   portfolios (higher HHI).
3. **Network proximity.** They appear closer to legal cartel anchors —
   shared tenders, shared buyers, shared cobidder networks.
4. **Bid-level behavior.** Their bid submissions exhibit patterns
   *consistent with* — but not diagnostic of — credible losing roles.

These differences give the loser-side adjacency target economic content and
make it harder to reduce the result to ordinary high-volume losing alone.

**Caveat.** The bid-level patterns are explicitly described as *consistent
with* credible losing roles, not as forensic proof. The manuscript
distinguishes consistency from diagnosticity and keeps the proof-producing
stage in the bid layer. The reading is 🟡 because the profile dimensions
are observational and within-stratum, sensitive to definitional choices
for buyer/product/network features.
